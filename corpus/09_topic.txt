Title: AI Research Document 09
Source: Research Corpus

Topic: Multimodal AI Models — Vision, Audio, and Beyond

Multimodal AI models can process and generate multiple types of data — text, images, audio, video, and code — within a unified architecture.

Key Multimodal Models (as of 2024-2025):
- GPT-4o: OpenAI's omni model handling text, images, and audio natively
- Claude 3.5 Sonnet: Anthropic's model with strong vision capabilities
- Gemini 1.5 Pro: Google's model with 1M context and video understanding
- LLaVA: Open-source visual language model
- Whisper: OpenAI's speech recognition model

Vision-Language Models (VLMs):
Combine a vision encoder (like CLIP or ViT) with a language decoder (LLM). The vision encoder converts images to embedding vectors that the LLM can process alongside text tokens.

CLIP (Contrastive Language-Image Pretraining):
Trained on 400M image-text pairs using contrastive learning. Learns a shared embedding space for images and text. Foundation for many VLMs.

Multimodal RAG:
Extends RAG to handle images, tables, charts, and documents alongside text. Approaches include:
- Storing image captions and using text retrieval
- Using multimodal embeddings (CLIP) for direct image retrieval
- Extracting text from images (OCR) before embedding

Text-to-Image Generation:
Diffusion models (DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion, Midjourney) generate images from text descriptions. Diffusion iteratively denoises a random noise tensor conditioned on text embeddings.

Audio Models:
- ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition): Whisper, wav2vec
- TTS (Text-to-Speech): ElevenLabs, Bark, Coqui
- Music generation: Suno, Udio

Multimodal agents can see screenshots, interact with UIs, read documents, and process audio — enabling computer use and robotic control applications.
