Source: Research Corpus

Topic: AI Safety and Alignment — Core Concepts

AI Safety is the field concerned with ensuring that AI systems behave safely, reliably, and in accordance with human values, especially as systems become more capable.

Key Alignment Problems:

Specification Problem: It is hard to formally specify what we want an AI system to do. Human values are complex, contextual, and sometimes contradictory. A reward function that seems correct can be gamed in unintended ways (specification gaming).

Robustness: AI systems should perform safely outside their training distribution. Current ML systems can fail catastrophically on out-of-distribution inputs.

Interpretability: We often cannot understand why a neural network made a specific decision. This 'black box' problem makes it hard to trust AI in high-stakes domains.

RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback):
A technique for aligning LLMs with human preferences. A reward model is trained on human preference comparisons, then used to fine-tune the LLM via reinforcement learning. Used by OpenAI (InstructGPT, GPT-4), Anthropic (Claude), and others.

Constitutional AI (CAI):
Anthropic's approach where the model critiques and revises its own outputs against a set of principles, reducing the need for human labeling of harmful content.

Red-teaming:
Deliberately trying to elicit harmful, biased, or dangerous behavior from AI systems to identify weaknesses before deployment.

AI Risk Categories:
- Misuse: Humans using AI for harmful purposes (deepfakes, disinformation, bioweapons)
- Misalignment: AI pursuing goals that differ from human intentions
- Structural: Concentration of power, economic disruption, erosion of privacy

Anthropic, DeepMind, OpenAI, and academic labs have dedicated safety research teams. The field is growing rapidly as model capabilities increase.
