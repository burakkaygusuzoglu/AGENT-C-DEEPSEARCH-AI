Title: ATS-Optimized Resume Guidelines for Tech & AI Roles
Source: Career Intelligence Corpus

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software used by companies to automatically parse, filter, and rank resumes before a human reviewer sees them. A resume that is not ATS-compatible may be rejected before anyone reads it. These guidelines focus on tech and AI/software engineering roles.

Why ATS Matters:
Large companies receive hundreds or thousands of applications per role. ATS filters based on keyword matching, section detection, and formatting compatibility. Estimates suggest 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS before reaching a recruiter.

1. Formatting Rules (Critical)

DO:
- Use a clean, single-column layout with standard section headings.
- Save and submit as PDF (preserves formatting) OR .docx if specifically requested.
- Use standard fonts: Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Georgia (10-12pt).
- Keep margins between 0.5" and 1".
- Use standard section titles: Experience, Education, Skills, Projects, Certifications.

DO NOT:
- Use tables, text boxes, headers/footers, or multi-column layouts (ATS can't parse them).
- Use graphics, icons, or charts.
- Put important information in headers or footers.
- Use creative section names like "My Journey" or "Superpowers."

2. Keyword Strategy

ATS ranks your resume by how well it matches the job description keywords.

Action steps:
- Copy the job description and identify the top technical skills mentioned (Python, Docker, LangChain, etc.)
- Mirror those exact terms in your resume (if you have the skill).
- Include both abbreviations and full forms: "NLP" and "Natural Language Processing."
- Add a dedicated "Technical Skills" section listing tools, languages, and frameworks.

3. Experience Section

Format each role as:
  [Job Title] | [Company Name] | [Month Year – Month Year]
  • Action verb + what you did + measurable result
  • Action verb + technology used + outcome achieved

Strong bullet examples:
  ✓ "Built a RAG pipeline using LangChain and ChromaDB, reducing manual research time by 60%."
  ✓ "Fine-tuned a BERT model on 10K labeled samples, achieving 94% F1-score on NER task."
  ✗ "Worked on AI projects and helped the team."

Use strong action verbs: Built, Developed, Deployed, Designed, Optimized, Automated, Reduced, Increased.

4. Projects Section (Crucial for Students)

Students with limited experience must emphasize projects heavily.

Format:
  [Project Name] | [Tech Stack] | [GitHub Link]
  • 2-3 bullets: what the project does + your contribution + result or scale.

Include:
- Personal projects from GitHub
- University capstone projects
- Hackathon projects
- Open-source contributions

5. Skills Section

Group by category for ATS clarity:
  Languages: Python, SQL, JavaScript
  Frameworks: LangChain, FastAPI, PyTorch, LangGraph
  Tools: Docker, Git, MLflow, ChromaDB
  Cloud: AWS (S3, Lambda), GCP (BigQuery)
  Concepts: RAG, Fine-tuning, Vector Search, CI/CD

6. Education Section

  [Degree] in [Field] | [University Name] | [Expected: Month Year]
  • GPA (if above 3.5/4.0 or equivalent)
  • Relevant coursework (Machine Learning, Data Structures, Algorithms)
  • Academic honors

7. One-Page Rule (For Students and Early-Career)

Keep it to one page until you have 5+ years of experience. Recruiters spend 7 seconds on average on an initial scan. Every line must earn its place.

8. Tailoring

Never submit the same generic resume everywhere. Keep a master resume with all experience, then tailor for each application by adjusting keywords and reordering bullet points to match the job description.

Tools That Help:
- Jobscan.co: compares your resume to a JD and gives ATS match score.
- Resume.io or Overleaf (LaTeX templates): clean ATS-compatible formats.
- Grammarly: proofreading.
