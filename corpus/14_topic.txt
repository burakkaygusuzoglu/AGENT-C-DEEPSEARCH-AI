Source: Research Corpus

Topic: AI for Scientific Discovery

AI is accelerating scientific discovery across biology, chemistry, physics, and materials science.

AlphaFold (DeepMind):
AlphaFold 2 (2020) achieved near-experimental accuracy in protein structure prediction, solving a 50-year grand challenge in biology. AlphaFold 3 (2024) extended to DNA, RNA, and small molecules. The AlphaFold Protein Structure Database contains predictions for 200M+ proteins.

Drug Discovery AI:
- Target identification: AI analyzes genomics data to find disease targets
- Molecule generation: Generative models (VAEs, diffusion) design new drug candidates
- ADMET prediction: Predict drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, toxicity
- Clinical trial optimization: AI matches patients to trials, predicts trial outcomes

Materials Science:
- Graph Neural Networks predict material properties from crystal structure
- Generative models design new materials with desired properties (e.g., better batteries)
- High-throughput screening: AI prioritizes which compounds to synthesize and test

Climate and Earth Science:
- GraphCast (Google): AI weather model, more accurate than traditional physics models
- Climate model emulators: Neural networks approximate expensive climate simulations
- Satellite imagery analysis: Detect deforestation, monitor ice sheets

Mathematics:
- AlphaProof and AlphaGeometry (DeepMind, 2024): Silver-medal level on International Math Olympiad problems
- LLMs assist with proof discovery and formalization

Key Techniques:
- Equivariant neural networks: Respect physical symmetries (rotation, translation)
- Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs): Incorporate physical laws as constraints
- Foundation models for science: Large pretrained models fine-tuned on scientific data
