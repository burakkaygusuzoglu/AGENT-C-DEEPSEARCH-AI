Source: Research Corpus

Topic: The Future of AI — Trends and Predictions 2025-2030

The AI landscape is evolving rapidly. Key trends shaping the next five years:

Agentic AI Mainstream Adoption:
AI agents that autonomously complete multi-step tasks are entering production. By 2026, analysts predict 80% of enterprise AI deployments will include agentic components. Key drivers: improved tool use, better planning, and lower error rates.

Multimodal Foundation Models:
Models natively handling text, image, audio, and video are becoming standard. Specialized modalities (3D, haptics, code, structured data) are being added. Omni models reduce the need for separate pipelines.

Reasoning and Planning:
OpenAI o1, o3 and similar 'thinking' models that spend more inference compute on reasoning show significant improvements on math, science, and coding benchmarks. Inference-time scaling is a major research direction.

AI in Software Development:
By 2027, predictions suggest AI will write 50-80% of new code. AI coding agents (Claude Code, Devin, GitHub Copilot Workspace) will handle larger and more complex tasks autonomously.

Smaller, More Efficient Models:
Quantization, distillation, and architecture improvements are making capable models run on consumer hardware. Models like Llama 3.2, Phi-3, and Mistral 7B demonstrate that small models can match much larger ones on many tasks.

AI Regulation:
EU AI Act fully in force by 2025. US AI legislation pending. China has its own AI regulations. Global standards are emerging but remain fragmented.

Open-Source AI Ecosystem:
Meta's Llama series, Mistral, and the HuggingFace ecosystem have created a strong open-source AI community. Open models are within months of frontier performance in many domains.

Embodied AI and Robotics:
Foundation models for robotics (RT-2, Figure AI, 1X) are enabling more general-purpose robots. The combination of vision-language models with physical control is advancing rapidly.

Key Risks to Monitor:
AI-generated disinformation at scale, job displacement in knowledge work, concentration of AI power in few companies, and catastrophic misuse scenarios.
