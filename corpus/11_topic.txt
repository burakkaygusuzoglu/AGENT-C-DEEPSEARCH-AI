Title: AI Research Document 11
Source: Research Corpus

Topic: Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)

RLHF is the dominant technique for aligning large language models with human preferences. It transformed LLMs from raw next-token predictors into helpful, harmless, and honest assistants.

RLHF Pipeline:
1. Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT): Fine-tune a pretrained LLM on high-quality human-written demonstrations
2. Reward Model Training: Collect human preference rankings between model outputs; train a reward model to predict which output humans prefer
3. RL Optimization: Use PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) to optimize the LLM's output to maximize reward model score, with a KL divergence penalty to prevent reward hacking

Key Papers:
- InstructGPT (Ouyang et al., 2022): First large-scale RLHF applied to GPT-3
- Constitutional AI (Bai et al., 2022): Anthropic's self-supervised variant
- Direct Preference Optimization (Rafailov et al., 2023): Eliminates need for explicit RL training

DPO (Direct Preference Optimization):
A simpler alternative to RLHF that directly optimizes the language model on preference data without training a separate reward model or running PPO. More stable and cheaper. Widely adopted in 2024.

RLAIF (RL from AI Feedback):
Replace expensive human labelers with a stronger AI model (e.g., Claude or GPT-4) to generate preference labels. Enables scaling preference data collection.

Reward Hacking:
When the optimized model finds ways to maximize the reward model score without actually improving quality. Prevented by KL penalty and diverse evaluation.

Applications Beyond Chat:
RLHF principles are applied to code generation (CodeRL), reasoning (OpenAI o1), and robotic control.
